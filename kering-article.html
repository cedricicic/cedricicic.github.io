Kering’s Lack of Lustre

An honest look into the company and the luxury market

Introduction

Kering is a well-established French-based corporation that owns prestigious luxury brands like Gucci, Bottega Veneta, and Alexander McQueen. Ranked 5th by market cap, they are one of the largest public luxury companies listed, partially responsible for shaping the current luxury landscape. However, the company has faced significant challenges in recent years, affecting its market performance and overall brand perception.

Current state

Since the online shopping frenzy during the pandemic, Kering has plummeted to half its peak at €730.8. Operating in a highly competitive market, the company faces numerous internal and external challenges that threaten its stability and growth

Internal problems by brand

  • Gucci: Kering’s flagship brand Gucci, is shifting its core aesthetics upon appointing a new creative director, Sabato De Sarno, last year. In line with the quiet luxury trend, the brand adopts a more minimalistic design rather than the known exuberant energy that Gucci brings. This shift has received negative feedback, particularly in the Chinese and Asia Pacific markets, leading to a 21% decline in revenue in the first quarter of 2024.

    Regarding investment, Kering has invested €1.3 billion and €885 million in two new retail locations for Gucci within six months. While these investments aim to explain Kering’s real estate portfolio, investors are sceptical, viewing them as distractions from addressing the disappointing financial performance.

  • Balenciaga: Known for their avant-garde and unconventional aesthetics, Balenciaga faced backlash for an ad campaign featuring children with teddy bears in bondage gear. The brand has struggled to recover from this scandal, relying heavily on sponsorships to maintain endorsements rather than rebranding.

  • Saint Laurent: Like many other heritage brands that started from haute couture, Saint Laurent faces challenges in balancing traditional artisan values with modern streetwear trends. The rise of fast fashion has further complicated this balance, leaving Saint Laurent in a difficult position within the luxury market.

External factors

  • Inflation: Although the luxury sector is almost free of pricing power, it is not recession-proof. Rising interest rates and the decline of the middle class have reduced nonessential spending, impacting Kering as consumers adopt more frugal lifestyles. Kering is a victim of many as it grapples with this stagnant demand.

  • The diminishing leather goods consumption market: The growing popularity of synthetic and cruelty-free products has directly affected Kering’s sales and revenue, as the market moves towards more sustainable options.

  • Losing its lustre: Now that luxury brands have become more accessible to a broader audience, the ultra-wealthy feel a loss of exclusivity that once defined their purchasing power. Thai shift drives the super-rich to seek more exclusive products and experiences, moving away from mainstream luxury brands.

How is LVMH on the rise under the same circumstance?

  • Broader market strategy: Instead of solely targeting the wealthy, LVMH covers a wide demographic by putting resources into Sephora, organised under the “perfumes and cosmetics” division. With affordable price tags and stores around the globe, they have built a solid relationship with these clients, turning each of them into a brand advocate.

  • Social responsibility: LVMH actively enhances its brand perception by investing in environmental protection, developing artisans' talents, and preserving traditional expertise. Notably, Louis Vuitton, LVMH's most profitable brand, crafted the medal trunks for the Olympic and Paralympic Games Paris 2024, showcasing their community involvement and garnering significant attention.

Conclusion

Kering faces sustainable challenges internally and externally, impacting its market performance and brand perception. To navigate these difficulties, Kering must address its internal brand issues, adapt to market movements and implement sound investments that align with long-term growth. By learning from competitors like LVMH, Kering can enhance its market position and regain its lustre.
